Databento Support for Nasdaq-100 Futures and
Options Data
E-mini Nasdaq-100 Futures (CME Globex symbol: NQ)
Databento does provide both real-time and historical data  for the E-mini Nasdaq-100 index futures. This
includes  specific  contract  months  such  as  NQU5  (Sep  2025)  and  NQZ5  (Dec  2025),  which  offer  direct
exposure to the Nasdaq-100 index. Key details of Databento’s futures data support: 
Availability:  Supported on CME Globex  – Databento is a licensed distributor of CME market data,
covering all CME futures  products. Historical data coverage goes back to 2010, and the same API
can be used for live (real-time) streaming as well as historical download/replay . In short, you can
access both intraday real-time feeds and deep historical archives for NQ futures via Databento. 
Data Types: Tick-by-tick trade and quote data  are provided, with the option to obtain full order
book depth  (all buy/sell orders at each price level) or top-of-book quotes as needed . Every
execution (trade) and every update to bids/asks is available, along with market statistics and
reference info from the CME feed . Data can be retrieved in various formats (e.g. CSV, JSON,
Parquet, or Databento’s binary format) and in different schemas (e.g. time & sales, order book
snapshots, OHLCV aggregates, etc.), according to the documentation. 
Granularity:  The futures data is highly granular , delivered at the tick level with nanosecond
timestamps  for each event . This means you get the exact timing and sequence of every
trade or quote update. Such precision allows for detailed analysis or backtesting on NQ futures.
Databento’s infrastructure ensures that the full tick history (including millions of ticks per day  for
active contracts) is available, and you can also request continuous contract series if you prefer to
handle contract rollovers seamlessly. 
Options on E-mini Nasdaq-100 Futures (Weekly & Quarterly Series)
Databento also supports market data for options on the E-mini Nasdaq-100 futures , encompassing all
the different expirations and series (weekly, end-of-month, and quarterly options on NQ futures). This
includes the recently introduced Monday and Wednesday weekly options (e.g. Q3C  for a Week-3 Wednesday
expiry) as well as the standard Friday/quarterly options on NQ. All these options contracts are listed on
CME’s Globex platform and are European-style  exercise (i.e. exercisable only at expiration) as specified by
CME . Key points for the options data: 
Supported Series: Every option contract on the NQ futures underlying is covered.  Databento’s
CME feed encompasses all Nasdaq-100 futures options – from the quarterly cycle options (root
symbol NQ) to weekly options  (including Monday & Wednesday weeklies like Q1A, Q3C, etc.), and
even end-of-month options (e.g. QNE) . In other words, whether it’s an option on the Sep 2025
NQ future or a Wednesday weekly that expires in between, Databento provides data for it. (Multi-
legged option spreads and combinations are included as well, per Databento documentation .) •

Data Types and Granularity:  The options data includes full tick-level detail  for trades and
quotes, similar to the futures. You can stream or download every trade, quote update, and order
book change for the options on NQ futures. Both top-of-book quotes and full market depth  are
available from the CME Globex feed . Historical coverage for these options aligns with their
introduction dates (CME’s feed data is available from 2010 onward, so all Nasdaq-100 options data
since launch is accessible). The data comes with high timestamp precision as well, allowing you to
analyze options markets with fine time resolution. Additionally, Databento’s API supports retrieving
entire option chains in one call (using “parent” symbology for the underlying), making it convenient
to get all strikes/expirations for , say, NQ weekly options at once . 
Implied Volatility/Greeks: Note that implied volatilities or Greeks are not provided as pre-
calculated fields  in Databento’s market data. According to Databento’s documentation, they do not
currently include  implied vol or option Greeks in the feed (to maintain data transparency and let
users derive these as needed) . Instead, you receive the raw option prices (bid/ask, trades) and
can calculate IV or Greeks yourself using an options pricing model (Databento even provides
examples, such as using the Black-76 model for index futures options). In summary, trades and
quotes for the options are available , but you’ll need to compute metrics like IV or Delta from that
data if required. 
Additional Data:  For options on futures, Databento does provide reference information  and end-
of-day statistics that can be useful. For example, you can query the instrument definitions to get 
option contract details  (expiration date, strike, underlying future, option type, etc.) and you can
retrieve open interest  and other stats via their APIs . This helps in identifying the exact option
contracts (such as all options on a given NQ futures contract like NQU5) and analyzing volume/open-
interest alongside the tick data. All of this is delivered under the CME Globex MDP 3.0 dataset on
Databento, ensuring consistency with how the futures data is accessed. 
Sources:  Databento  official  documentation  and  product  pages  confirm  support  for  CME  E-mini
Nasdaq-100 futures and options  data. The CME Globex MDP 3.0 feed (which Databento offers) includes “all
futures and options on CME… [with] full book, stats, reference data” , and Databento explicitly states that it
covers options on futures  (CME and others) in its offerings . The platform provides tick-level, depth-of-
market data  for these instruments  with nanosecond timestamps, available both in real-time streaming
and via historical download. However , “Databento [doesn’t] provide pre-calculated implied volatility or Greeks”
for options, so users would derive those from the price data if needed . For reference, CME’s own
specifications note that the Monday/Wednesday weekly Nasdaq-100 options (e.g. Q3C) are European-style
expirations on the nearest NQ future , and all such weekly and monthly option series on NQ are included
in Databento’s data coverage.
