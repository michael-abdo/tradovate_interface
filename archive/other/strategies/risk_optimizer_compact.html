Optimal Strategy:

▼ Why This Strategy? Understanding the Math

The Core Insight:

When setups are limited but accounts are cheap relative to income, maximizing attempt frequency beats maximizing individual survival.

Time Arbitrage:

You're trading - hours of work for a -% chance to save - days of trading. That's a -x return on time invested.

The Formula:

Optimal f = maximize(f × P(success|f)) where:

  • f = front-load factor (position size multiplier)
  • P(success|f) = probability of reaching target before drawdown
  • Higher f → fewer trades needed but lower survival probability
Why Aggressive Works Here:
  • Limited setups (1/day) → opportunity cost dominates
  • Cheap accounts ($60) → low replacement cost
  • Decent income ($60/hr) → can afford multiple attempts
  • Positive expectancy (51% @ 1:1) → math favors more attempts
